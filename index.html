About the Workshop

Bridging Climate Science, Health & Farmer Livelihoods

This international workshop convenes researchers from Japan, Thailand, Vietnam, the Philippines, and Singapore to address one of the most pressing challenges facing Asian agriculture: how to restructure sustainable income bases for rice farmers under intensifying climate change. Through pilot survey reports, solution-oriented presentations, and a collaborative panel discussion, we explore the concept of “value-bundling” — integrating climate adaptation measures with market and health values to create meaningful economic incentives for farmers.

Speakers

Our Researchers

MY
Organizer · Moderator

Prof. Miki Yoshizumi

College of Gastronomy Management, Ritsumeikan University, Japan

Ph.D. in Global Environmental Studies from Kyoto University. Former Research Assistant at the UN Centre for Regional Development and Associate Professor at Kyoto University. Her research focuses on food sustainability, sustainable regional development utilizing food resources, and ESD (Education for Sustainable Development) for community leader training, conducting collaborative fieldwork across domestic and international sites.

MM
Co-Organizer · Speaker

Assoc. Prof. Marvin Joseph F. Montefrio

Dept. of Sociology & Anthropology, National University of Singapore

Ph.D. in Environmental and Natural Resources Policy from SUNY-ESF with an MPA from the Maxwell School, Syracuse University. Faculty fellow of the Food Politics and Society Cluster at NUS’s Asia Research Institute. An interdisciplinary scholar employing ethnographic and quantitative approaches, his research explores the political economy and cultural politics of food sustainability and climate change impacts on food security in Southeast Asia.

OS
Speaker

Dr. Orawan Srisompun

Faculty of Technology, Mahasarakham University, Thailand

Assistant Professor specializing in agricultural economics and agricultural extension. Her research examines factors influencing farmers’ adoption of sustainable cultivation practices, including organic cassava and rice farming in Thailand’s northeastern Isan region, with expertise in crop production systems and agricultural policy evaluation.

HA
Speaker

Dr. Hoang Ha Anh

Faculty of Economics, Nong Lam University, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

Lecturer at the Department of Agricultural, Resources and Environmental Economics. Ph.D. in Agricultural Economics from Northwest A&F University, China. His research employs system dynamics modeling to analyze environmental economics challenges, including mangrove–shrimp ecosystems, drought vulnerability, and sustainable agricultural development across Vietnam’s Mekong Delta and Central Highlands.

TH
Speaker

Dr. Thanh Tam Ho

College of International Relations, Ritsumeikan University, Japan

Assistant Professor at the College of International Relations, Ritsumeikan University. Ph.D. in Economics from Ritsumeikan University; B.Sc. in Environmental Economics from Nong Lam University. Former lecturer at Nong Lam University and postdoctoral researcher at Ritsumeikan’s Asia-Japan Research Organization. His research investigates agricultural sustainability and climate resilience — analyzing adaptation and mitigation strategies, farmer behavior towards sustainable rice farming, and agricultural policy effectiveness across Japan and Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Thailand.

QT
Speaker

Dr. Quoc Thinh Tran

College of Life Sciences & Japan Biochar Research Center, Ritsumeikan University, Japan

Assistant Professor in the Department of Bioengineering. Ph.D. in Environmental Science from Okayama University. His research aims to find optimal soil environments for agriculture by focusing on soil biological, chemical, and physical properties — particularly total bacterial counts as soil quality indicators. He also studies microbial activity related to nitrogen and phosphorus cycling, and the application of biochar for carbon sequestration and soil improvement.
